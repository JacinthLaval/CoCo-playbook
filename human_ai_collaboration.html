Four Modes of Human-AI Interaction

Over the course of building the Mississippi Fetal & Infant Mortality Review (FIMR) system, a clear collaboration pattern emerged. The human drives what and why; the AI handles how. But the human stays in the loop through four distinct interaction modes — including the discipline of pausing to codify hard-won patterns into reusable skills.

The Spark
The entire project begins. Todd connects a real-world Mississippi legislative mandate to existing Snowflake healthcare data assets, and sketches the full vision in a single message.
Human Prompt
"morning! I've been asked to look at the following situation in Mississippi: The State Department of Health would be charged with creating a medical panel to review the state's high fetal and infant mortality rate while examining deaths of infants up to one year of age. Take a look at the synthetic healthcare data share... is there pediatric and fetal data in it? If not, thinking using that structure to create some robust synthetic data to create a cortex analyst view and potentially link PubMed and other assets"
Result: 12-table OMOP CDM dataset with 50K pregnancies, Mississippi-specific demographics, 77 county locations, and linked SDOH data from CDC, USDA, and HRSA.
Pushing Beyond Convention
The AI had proposed using standard OHDSI prediction patterns. Todd rejects the conventional approach and pushes toward something novel — a multi-modal deep learning model blending structured clinical data with unstructured SDOH.
Human Prompt
"so..... before we go too far, we are basically taking what others have done... would our prior ontology pattern take this to another level? how do we go about this prediction in a novel way? blend some unstructured social determinant of health data? see where I'm going, a really deep model?"
Result: A novel multi-modal Transformer with dual encoders (mother + infant branches), bidirectional cross-attention, and a gated SDOH fusion layer — 1.3M parameters achieving 0.893 AUROC.
Lateral Thinking
With the technical system built, Todd makes an unexpected lateral connection — Mississippi's dramatic literacy turnaround — as a proven blueprint for tackling infant mortality through the same cultural and policy levers.
Human Prompt
"awesome... last item for tonight.. take a look at the fantastic success in Mississippi of their advancement in literacy and overall K-12 education system. What were the cultural changes that had to take place by the parents to make this change? How did they do it? I bet you know where I'm going here.... do some research that I don't need to constantly approve."
Result: "From Reading to Breathing" — an 8-pillar policy playbook mapping Mississippi's literacy miracle (49th→21st in NAEP reading) to a proposed infant mortality reduction framework, with evidence from Cradle Cincinnati's 41% IMR reduction.
The Full Stack Vision
A single message that defines the entire application architecture: React dashboard, SPCS hosting, interactive county maps, NLP querying, Cortex Agent with PubMed, and multi-tasking instructions.
Human Prompt
"ok, let's build a react app hosted in SPCS accessible via a new PAT that accesses this. We'll add TN data later. Looking for an interactive visual that allows you to drill into county/city/zip specific statistics filtering by year/month and race as a general requirement. Open to suggestions. Also want the ask your natural language option for ad-hoc analysis. If you want to multi-task, need to create the cortex agent that will add the Pubmed Cortex Search service to the FIMR analyst."
Result: A full SPCS-deployed React dashboard with county choropleth maps, KPI cards, trend charts, a Cortex Agent "Ask" panel wired to both FIMR analytics and 72M PubMed abstracts, plus a real-time risk scoring tab.
Providing Clarity & Direction
The human steers decisions. The AI presents options; the human chooses the path.
Data Precision
The AI had calculated Mississippi birth numbers. Todd injects real epidemiological data and specifies exact mortality trend requirements, plus plans ahead for a comparison state.
Human Prompt
"thinking right... also want the data to reflect the actual mortality rate of 9.7 out of 1000 in 2024 growing from 8 in 1000 in 2020. let's do 50k pregnancies with that 6% decrease trend line. after we get MS looking good, will want to add another state that is doing much better, maybe TN, to look at hypothetical differences and postulate potential root causes."
Result: The 50K dataset was generated with year-specific birth counts (35K→32.9K), a realistic 8.0→9.7 IMR trend over 2020-2025, and clear racial disparities matching published CDC statistics.
Domain Expertise Injection
Just before the data generator was about to run, Todd stops the process to inject a critical racial disparity statistic that would be invisible to the AI.
Human Prompt
"before you do that, let me add one macro statistic that is true in the real data.... the african american or black mortality rate was 15.2 compared to other races 5.8 in 2024. while it does not need to be perfectly aligned, need to show the higher rate amongst african americans"
Result: Generated data reflects Black IMR of 13.6/1000 vs. White 4.5/1000 — accurately modeling the 2.6x racial disparity observed in real Mississippi vital statistics.
Deployment Decision
The AI presented three deployment architecture options. Todd makes a decisive choice and simultaneously defines a new feature requirement: the patient generator.
Human Prompt
"let's do Option B first for demonstration purposes adding a 'new patient data' generator to simulate a new pregnant mother entering the program."
Result: Inference engine integrated directly into the Flask backend, with a synthetic PatientGenerator supporting low/moderate/high risk profiles across 15 Mississippi counties. Deployed as a new "Risk Score" tab with interactive gauges.
Priority Setting
Multiple next steps were available (ontology, PubMed, predictions). A single short directive sets the immediate priority.
Human Prompt
"let's do the semantic view first"
Result: Full semantic view deployed as MS_FIMR_ANALYTICS with 12 tables, 17 relationships, and 10 verified queries — enabling natural language analytics across the entire OMOP dataset.
Correcting Misunderstandings
The human catches what the AI misses. Short feedback loops prevent compounding errors.
Catching a Critical Bug
The Risk Score tab had just been deployed. It looked beautiful. But Todd's systematic testing — generating multiple patients across all risk levels — immediately revealed the model wasn't actually differentiating.
Human Prompt
"functional and beautiful. one question. have generated five new records, some low, some moderate, and some high. all have come back at moderate overall risk"
Root Cause Found: Feature vectors were being constructed in a different column order than the model was trained on. Polars pivot operations produce nondeterministic column orders between runs. The AI traced back through normalization statistics to reconstruct the exact training order and fixed the generator.
Wrong Target
The AI found a Streamlit app and started analyzing it. Todd immediately redirects — it's not the right application.
Human Prompt
"not the streamlit app, look at the react-based app that has the tabs, ER Console, Similarity, TRE/OMOP, and Settings. its the TRE/OMOP tab that looks to be connected to an Analyst agent"
Course Corrected: The AI pivoted to the correct React application, identified its architecture, and used it as the foundation for the new FIMR dashboard — inheriting the proven SPCS deployment pattern.
Quality Standards
The dashboard had been iterating on a county bubble map using Carto tiles. Todd steps back and challenges both the visual quality and the framework choice.
Human Prompt
"let's take a step back... what is the framework being used in this react app. I must say it's not near as attractive as the precision medicine app. also, is there not a maps framework that can be used other than carto, some other python package?"
Result: Complete UI overhaul — switched from Carto bubble maps to react-simple-maps with TopoJSON for a proper county choropleth, rewrote all 8 components with a new Snowflake-blue design system, card shadows, and the polished light theme.
Precision Feedback
The map was rendering after a query fix, but the visual encoding had a bug. Five words is all it takes.
Human Prompt
"bubble size and color don't match"
Fixed: SVG layer ordering corrected so bubble radius scaled with IMR magnitude while color mapped to the same metric — a 5-word correction that identified a rendering bug the AI would not have caught on its own.
Pausing to Codify
The human recognizes when a hard-won pattern deserves to become a reusable skill — not just a one-off solution.
From Notes to Formal Knowledge
After building a 3-layer defense-in-depth guardrails system (read-only Snowflake role, API-layer SQL validation, UI confirmation flow), the AI had documented the pattern in a markdown file. Todd sees something bigger.
Human Prompt
"i see you created an md file for healthcare guardrails. should we formally make that a skill?"
Skill Created: agent-guardrails — a reusable skill that implements defense-in-depth security for any Cortex Agent or MCP Server app, ensuring AI-generated queries can never modify data.
Capturing Hard-Won Lessons
The session had just hit every pitfall of bulk data loading into Snowflake: multi-value INSERT failures at scale, Python date serialization issues, distribution stacking. Todd pauses the build to preserve what was learned.
Human Prompt
"before running, looks like you've learned a few things about generating synthetic data at scale here, should you create a skill or will this become a best practice inherently?"
Skill Created: synthetic-data-generator — captures proven patterns for generating synthetic data at scale and loading into Snowflake, avoiding the write_pandas vs. INSERT pitfalls, date serialization issues, and distribution management.
Turning Frustration into Future-Proofing
After days of debugging SPCS authentication — discovering that the service token does NOT work with the SQL REST API, only with the Python connector — Todd signals that this pain should never be repeated.
Human Prompt
"OK, let's continue. Seems to be a very difficult process for auth between a containerized app and the data. Once we figure this out, will want to create a skill"
Skill Created: spcs-spa-auth — the definitive SPCS authentication pattern: use snowflake-connector-python with the injected service token, never the SQL REST API. Prevents others from repeating a multi-day debugging journey.
Recognizing the Repeatable Pattern
The FIMR dashboard was live on SPCS with Cortex Agent integration. Todd zooms out and sees the deployment pattern itself — React SPA + Flask backend + Cortex Agent via DATA_AGENT_RUN — as a reusable template.
Human Prompt
"so react apps to cortex agents, worth a skill for repeatable consistency?"
Skill Created: spcs-react-agent — end-to-end recipe for React SPA dashboards on SPCS with Cortex Agent integration, covering service token auth, DATA_AGENT_RUN vs REST API, Docker amd64 builds, ALTER SERVICE deployment, and nginx proxy configuration.

Key Takeaways

The Human Sets the "Why"

Every breakthrough in this project — the multi-modal architecture, the literacy playbook, the SDOH fusion — came from the human. The AI never would have connected Mississippi education reform to infant mortality reduction.

Short Loops Beat Long Specs

Five words ("bubble size and color don't match") corrected a rendering bug faster than any design document could. The most effective corrections were the shortest ones, applied at the moment of discovery.

Domain Knowledge Is Irreplaceable

The AI built the PyTorch model. But knowing that Black infant mortality is 2.6x higher, or that normal_pregnancy should be a count not a flag — that required a human who understands the clinical domain.

Codify the Pain

Four times during this project, the human paused forward progress to turn hard-won lessons into reusable skills. The frustration of SPCS auth debugging became a skill that saves the next team days. Institutional knowledge compounds.
